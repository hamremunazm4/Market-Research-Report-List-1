<p><p>The Copper Phosphorus Alloy (CuP) market comprises various grades, primarily CuP8, CuP10, and CuP15, each distinguished by their phosphorus content. CuP8, with 8% phosphorus, offers good brazing properties, making it ideal for joining copper and brass components. CuP10 contains 10% phosphorus, enhancing fluid resistance and strength in applications. CuP15, with 15% phosphorus, is used for higher-temperature applications. The "Other" category includes specialty alloys and variations tailored for specific industrial needs, expanding the versatility of CuP products.</p></p>
