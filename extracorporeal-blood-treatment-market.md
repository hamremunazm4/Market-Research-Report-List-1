<p><p>The extracorporeal blood treatment market encompasses various techniques for treating blood outside the body. Hemodialysis is primarily used for chronic kidney failure, filtering waste and excess fluids from the blood. Acute dialysis serves patients with sudden kidney issues, providing immediate renal replacement therapy. Apheresis involves separating blood components, often for therapeutic purposes like treating autoimmune diseases or removing toxins. Each type caters to specific medical needs, driving demand for advanced technologies and improving patient outcomes in renal and hematological treatments.</p></p>
