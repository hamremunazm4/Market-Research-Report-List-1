<p><p>The intrauterine devices (IUD) market comprises two main types: hormonal IUDs and copper IUDs. Hormonal IUDs release progestin, which thickens cervical mucus and prevents ovulation, offering effective pregnancy prevention for several years. In contrast, copper IUDs utilize copper's spermicidal properties to prevent fertilization and can remain effective for up to a decade. Both types provide long-term, reversible contraception but differ in their mechanisms of action, side effects, and duration of effectiveness, catering to diverse user preferences and needs.</p></p>
