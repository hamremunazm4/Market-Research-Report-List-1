<p><p>An Optical Fiber Gyroscope (OFG) is a type of gyroscopic sensor that uses the principles of fiber optics to measure angular velocity. It operates based on the Sagnac effect, which occurs when light travels in opposite directions around a coiled fiber. The phase difference between the two light beams is indicative of the rotation rate, making OFGs highly sensitive and accurate. These devices find applications in various sectors, including aerospace, automotive, and navigation systems, due to their reliability and ability to function in extreme conditions.</p><p>The Optical Fiber Gyroscope Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.2% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by the increasing demand for advanced navigation and guidance systems, along with the rise in autonomous vehicles and drones. Additionally, the trend toward miniaturization of electronic components opens new avenues for OFG integration into compact systems. Innovations in materials and manufacturing processes also continue to enhance the performance and reduce the costs of optical fiber gyroscopes. As industries seek precision and reliability, the adoption of OFGs is set to expand significantly, positioning them as critical components in next-generation technological applications.</p></p>

<p><p>The Optical Fiber Gyroscope (OFG) market can be categorized into three main types: Interferometric, Resonant, and Other. </p><p>Interferometric gyroscopes utilize interference patterns of light to measure rotational movement, offering high precision and stability. Resonant gyroscopes are based on the principles of resonance to detect rotation, often providing compact designs and lower costs. The "Other" category encompasses various emerging technologies and hybrid systems that may blend features of the primary types, catering to diverse applications in aerospace, automotive, and robotics industries.</p></p>
