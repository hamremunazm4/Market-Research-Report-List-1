<p><p>Primary cell culture refers to the process of isolating and growing cells directly from living tissues. Unlike established cell lines, which are immortalized and can be maintained for extended periods, primary cells retain their physiological and morphological characteristics, making them invaluable for research, drug development, and toxicology studies.
